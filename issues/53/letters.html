Thanks for mentioning my brother | “Do You Speak English?” hb 5.1

I was just surfing the net, and I guess I just stumbled on this site. What really got my attention was the article “Do You Speak English” written by Kevin Lee in hardboiled 5.1. Because I am also an older sister of one of the Asian American youths that was a part of the incident.

The feature of this article took me by surprise because I didn’t realize that there were so many people that were aware of what happened. I was so shocked myself when my brother told me what had happened to him. I never would have thought that this type of racism ever existed in Seattle because I have never personally experienced any type of blatant racism against me. I was not only angry, but I also felt that it had major negative effects on my brother and his friends. He started to distrust anyone that wasn’t Asian or Asian American, and also began to become very anxious near Caucasian policemen.

Anyhow, I have been really impressed at how the APA community in not only Seattle, but nationally as well, in handling the situation.

Well, anyways, I just wanted to say thanks for featuring that article in your mag!

P.S. The two policemen involved only received a suspension for “misconduct” and NOT racial profiling . . . Although he implied that he had “killed people” like us during the Vietnam War . . ..

-web reader

First of all, i thought Richard Lee’s Bay Area massage parlor expose was outstanding. Please tell him so, and any other reporters who had the temerity to venture into massage parlors and query prostitutes.
