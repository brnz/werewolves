Nature Calls

lisa wong macabasco


Lisa Ling fans (both of them) will now have to turn the channel to MSNBC to get their fix of the journalist-Old Navy spokesmodel-Rick Yune arm decoration.

Ling is leaving ABC’s “The View” after three years for greener pastures, mountains, waterfalls, and forests on MSNBC’s “National Geographic Explorer,” according to the Nov. 19 issue of The New York Post. Ling said that her decision was not due to any animosity between herself and her sofa mates Barbara, Star, Meredith, and Joy, but rather to her desire to travel and pursue international reporting, the Post reports.

The move to “Explorer” means that Ling’s audience will drop from 2.6 million viewers a day to about 400,000 viewers once a week (Sunday evening). However, Ling hopes she can increase the show’s viewership and calls the gig her “dream job.”

“My fantasy would be to do for international stories what Oprah [Winfrey] has done for books, and I’ve got a long way to go,” she said, as fans of good literature everywhere interpreted that comment to mean that international news stories will become oversentimental, cheesy, and predictable.

“I really feel that my generation, in particular, is facing a crisis - we are, one day, going to inherit the problems of the world and we’re ill-equipped to do that, which scares me,” she told the “Post,” pulling on a pair of Old Navy capris and hopping on the back of a scooter.
